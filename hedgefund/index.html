What is a Hedge Fund?

In simple terms, Investors pool their money into the fund so Vestrax can use said money to purchase assets, stocks, bonds, derivatives, etc. (anything that makes money) to turn a profit. This profit is then redistributed to all the investors based on the % investment they contributed. Vestrax takes a portion of the profits and withdrawal as part of its fees (2% of Assets/20% of profits) (Based on the standard fees that Hedge Funds take irl). Vestrax may restrict individuals who wish to withdraw. (This is to prevent liquidity issues in fund from occurring)

Can I Lose Money?

Yes, like every other investment in life, Vestrax Hedge Fund can incur losses and not turn a profit. We are very open that losses can occur and should be considered when putting money into our fund. Remember, you are risking money to make more money. Same goes for investing into banks, banks can go insolvent, bad management, etc. However, unlike banks and other firms, we are open that there is a risk and don't hide our losses by printing money or betting on more deposits.

How is the Fund's Value Calculated?

The Hedge Fund's value is the current net asset value that Vestrax has at its disposal. It does not take into account that some assets may not be able to liquidated easily.
